Conceptual Model

In our conceptual model, we translate the theoretical model into a higher level of abstraction, that allows us to visually show the relationship between different entities and resources. The existing widely known standards of DC/DCterms and CIDOC-CRM are the most used, together with Schema.org, that proved to be decisive to identify some specific class of items. Other useful employed ontologies were FRBRoo and DBpedia, mainly used for describing more general aspects, and Music Ontology, Lido and Premis, strictly connected to some detailed types of items, like musical production and digital object.

Taking into account the suggested division by "Who", "When", "Where" and "What", we produce the tables below, with 8 subcategories:

Who: People
Where: Place
When: Date
What: Visual artwork, Audio object, Website, Written document, Event

The "Who", which involves the concept of "Person" and "Corporate Body", results to be the broader category. The table reflects the reality of the situation, in which the role of governments, institutions and single actors makes the difference. Both the places linked to the production of the items and the building of Tora prison convey in the "Where" category. As for this latter, also "When" mainly depends on the specific release or display date of the objects, like the audios, the artworks and the website. The "Date" class has also the responsibility to show the starting period of the Patrick detention, that has not yet an "endDate". In the "What" section, split into the five macro-categories of the items, we decided to deepen the details that characterized them.
